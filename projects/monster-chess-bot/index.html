Monster Chess Bot

A from-scratch, dependency-free Rust engine for Monster Chess, built to overtake the strongest public Monster bots on playstrategy.org.

The variant

Monster Chess is wildly asymmetric. White gets only a king and the c2–f2 pawns, but plays two moves every turn; Black keeps the full standard army and plays one. You win by capturing the enemy king, and a side with no legal move loses — there are no stalemates. White may even pass through check on his first move, as long as the turn ends with the king safe.

Those twists break most standard chess-engine assumptions: “check” means capturable across an entire two-move turn, en passant works asymmetrically for the two sides, and the branching factor of White’s move pairs explodes compared to normal chess.

The engine

  • A complete Monster rules layer — compound-turn move generation, legality, castling, and both en passant quirks — backed by perft and regression tests.
  • Search over whole turns rather than single plies: iterative-deepening alpha-beta with a transposition table, aspiration windows, PVS-style re-searches, killer/history move ordering, and a bounded quiescence layer for captures, promotions, and forced evasions.
  • A handcrafted Monster-specific evaluation: white king pressure and its two-move capture radius, black king shelter, promotion races, and tempo.
  • A tactical regression suite of Monster motifs — two-move king hunts, promotion-capture mates, and quiet preparatory first moves.

Status

Written in Rust with zero external crates. The engine plays locally today; up next are a self-play match harness and the PlayStrategy connector to take it online against the reigning public bots.
